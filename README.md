# Verbal De-escalation Trainer for Schools

An interactive web application designed to help school staff practice and improve verbal de-escalation techniques using realistic school-based scenarios.

## Overview

This trainer provides realistic scenarios where staff members practice responding to student verbal aggression and escalating behavior. The application evaluates responses in real-time, provides feedback on de-escalation effectiveness, and teaches evidence-based CPI (Crisis Prevention Institute) techniques.

## Features

### 📋 Interactive Scenarios
- **6 Common School Situations:**
  - Student refusing to follow instructions
  - Student upset after being corrected
  - Confrontation during classroom transition
  - Student late for class (multiple times)
  - Peer conflict escalating between students
  - Student frustrated with assignment difficulty

- **Progressive Escalation:** Scenarios escalate based on user responses, showing realistic student behavior progression
- **Two Possible Outcomes:** Success (student calms down) or Escalation (student becomes physically aggressive)

### 📊 Real-Time Feedback
- Evaluates responses based on de-escalation techniques
- Highlights what was done well
- Identifies areas for improvement
- Suggests better approaches
- Tracks escalation level visually

### 📚 Learning Resources
- **About De-escalation:** Understanding verbal de-escalation principles
- **Key CPI Principles:** Respect, Listening, Calm Voice, Non-threatening, Empathy, Choices
- **6 Core Techniques:**
  1. Validation
  2. Empathetic Listening
  3. Offering Choices
  4. Identifying the Need
  5. Setting Limits with Respect
  6. Using Calming Language

### 📈 Progress Tracking
- Completion of all scenarios
- Success/failure outcomes
- Average effectiveness score
- Scenario-by-scenario breakdown

## How to Use

### Getting Started
1. Click **"Start Training"** to begin the scenario practice
2. Read the scenario situation carefully
3. You'll see the student's initial escalating behavior

### During Each Scenario
1. Read the student's statement and the escalation level
2. Type your response in the text box - focus on:
   - Using a calm, respectful tone
   - Validating feelings
   - Listening actively
   - Offering choices
   - Avoiding power struggles
3. Click **"Submit Response"** to see how the student reacts
4. The escalation meter updates based on your response quality
5. The scenario continues with the student's next response

### Understanding Feedback
- **✓ Green:** You did something right - keep it up!
- **✗ Red:** This approach escalated the situation - try a different technique
- **💡 Yellow:** Tips and best practices for the scenario

### Outcomes
- **Calmed Down:** De-escalation was successful - student's behavior improved
- **Physical Aggression:** Escalation occurred - student became physically aggressive

## De-escalation Techniques

### 1. **Validation**
Acknowledge the person's feelings and concerns without necessarily agreeing with their behavior.
- *Example:* "I can see this is really frustrating for you. I want to help."

### 2. **Empathetic Listening**
Listen without interrupting. Show you understand their perspective.
- *Example:* "Tell me what happened. I'm listening."

### 3. **Offering Choices**
Give options to help them feel in control and respected.
- *Example:* "Would you like to step outside for a moment, or talk here?"

### 4. **Identifying the Need**
Determine what the person really needs: respect, safety, control, or understanding.
- *Example:* "It seems like you feel disrespected. Let me listen."

### 5. **Setting Limits with Respect**
Establish boundaries while maintaining the person's dignity.
- *Example:* "I respect your feelings, but I need you to speak respectfully to me too."

### 6. **Using Calming Language**
Choose words and tone that soothe rather than provoke.
- *Example:* "Let's take a breath and figure this out together."

## Key Principles

### What to DO ✓
- Use a calm, steady tone
- Maintain open, non-threatening body language
- Listen actively and show empathy
- Validate feelings first
- Offer choices and autonomy
- Take time to understand
- Show genuine concern
- Maintain respectful boundaries

### What to AVOID ✗
- Raising your voice
- Matching their intensity or anger
- Being defensive or dismissive
- Making threats or ultimatums
- Invading personal space
- Arguing or trying to prove them wrong
- Ignoring emotional needs
- Using sarcasm or disrespect

## Evaluation Criteria

Your responses are evaluated based on:

1. **Tone & Language:** Calm, respectful, non-threatening
2. **Empathy:** Showing understanding and validation
3. **Listening:** Asking questions, seeking to understand
4. **Problem-solving:** Offering solutions and choices
5. **De-escalation:** Moving toward calm resolution

## Best Practices for Training

1. **Practice Regularly:** Complete the scenarios multiple times to internalize techniques
2. **Reflect:** After each scenario, consider how you might handle similar situations differently
3. **Learn from Failures:** When de-escalation fails, notice what could have been said instead
4. **Apply:** Use these techniques in actual classroom situations
5. **Seek Support:** Discuss challenging scenarios with colleagues and administrators
