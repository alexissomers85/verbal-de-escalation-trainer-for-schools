About Verbal De-escalation

What is Verbal De-escalation?

Verbal de-escalation is the use of words, tone, and communication techniques to reduce a person's anger or aggression without physical intervention.

Key CPI Principles

🎯 Respect

Show genuine care and concern for the person's feelings and perspective.

👂 Listening

Listen actively to understand the root cause of the escalation.

💬 Calm Voice

Maintain a calm, steady tone regardless of the student's behavior.

🚫 Non-threatening

Use open body language and maintain appropriate distance.

🤝 Empathy

Validate feelings and show understanding of their situation.

✨ Choices

Offer choices to help the person feel more in control.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Raising your voice or matching their intensity
  • ❌ Being defensive or dismissive of their concerns
  • ❌ Making threats or ultimatums
  • ❌ Invading personal space
  • ❌ Arguing or proving them wrong
  • ❌ Ignoring their emotional needs

De-escalation Techniques

1. Validation

What: Acknowledge the person's feelings and concerns.

Example: "I can see this is really frustrating for you. I want to help."

2. Empathetic Listening

What: Listen without interrupting and show you understand.

Example: "Tell me what happened. I'm listening."

3. Offering Choices

What: Give options to help them feel in control.

Example: "Would you like to step outside for a moment, or talk here?"

4. Identifying the Need

What: Determine what the person really needs (respect, safety, control).

Example: "It seems like you feel disrespected. Let me listen."

5. Setting Limits with Respect

What: Establish boundaries while maintaining respect.

Example: "I respect your feelings, but I need you to speak respectfully to me too."

6. Using Calming Language

What: Choose words that soothe rather than provoke.

Example: "Let's take a breath and figure this out together."
